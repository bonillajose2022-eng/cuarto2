Sequencing Words

Learn how to put events in order!

📖 What are Sequencing Words?

We use sequencing words to explain the order of events or steps. They help our reader understand what happens first, second, and last!

🥇 First ➡️ Next ⏩ Then 🔄 After that 🏁 Finally
Example — How to make a sandwich:
First, take two slices of bread. Next, add butter. Then, put cheese and ham. After that, close the sandwich. Finally, enjoy your meal! 🥪

Simple Connectives

And, But, Because — joining ideas together!

📖 What are Connectives?

Connectives join two ideas or sentences together. Let's learn three important ones!

AND

Adds information

"I like cats and dogs."
BUT

Shows contrast / difference

"I like cats but I don't like dogs."
BECAUSE

Gives a reason / cause

"I like cats because they are cute."

Zero Conditional

If + present simple → present simple

📖 What is the Zero Conditional?

We use the Zero Conditional to talk about things that are always true — facts, science, nature, and cause & effect!

IF + present simple , present simple
🌍 Environmental Examples:
🌡️ If you heat water, it boils.
🌊 If people pollute rivers, fish die.
🌲 If we plant trees, the air becomes cleaner.
☀️ If the sun shines, ice melts.
💡 Tip: You can also start with the result: "Water boils if you heat it."

Irregular Verbs: BE & HAVE

Master the most important verbs in English!

📖 The Verb BE

SubjectPresentPast
Iamwas
You / We / Theyarewere
He / She / Itiswas

📖 The Verb HAVE

SubjectPresentPast
I / You / We / Theyhavehad
He / She / Ithashad
Examples:
🐶 My dog is very playful. Yesterday it was sick.
🎒 I have a new backpack. Last year I had an old one.
👫 We are good friends. We were classmates last year.
